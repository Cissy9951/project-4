ReReader

Design Process

Based on the property of this material, which is a dye, I had several initial ideas:

I eventually decided on using the dye to create reprintable books, because:

  1. To make the "recoloring" process of this dye easy for users, the ideal object is something with only 2 sides, and no hidden faces. A book, open or closed, fits this criteria.


  2. This dye will slowing transition from colorful to black under ambient light, which would create a lot of usability problems in other situations. However, the unopened pages of a book are protected from ambient light, and a reader likely would not stay on one opened page for several hours to let the dye fully transition to black.


  3. Printed books have their physicality that readers prefer over digital books, but printed books also use a lot of storage space, paper, and ink. Meanwhile, a reprogrammable ink has a lot of potential for reusability, and works well to resolve some of the issues with printed books.

After determining the application, I began brianstorming what are the objects that need to be designed for this applicaiotn. I started from epxloring hwat the printer might look like:

I believe instead of having a box like structure, this printer that reprints books would be more interesting if it has an irregular geometric shape.

I also thought about how the book might be printed inside the printer, and was inspired by the nozzle movement of 3D printers and laser cutters. The printer should also flip book pages automatically, and that could be achieved by inserting magnetic pieces to the corner of each book page, and have a magnetic foot inside the printer that picks up the corners, moves to the other side, and releases the corner.
